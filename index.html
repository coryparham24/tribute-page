Abraham Lincoln

Abraham Lincoln was the 16th president of the United States. His most notable accomplishment was his contribution to the freedom of slaves in America. He was assassinated by John Wilkes Booth on April 14, 1865.

Following is a timeline of Abraham Lincoln's life:

  • February 12, 1809 - Born in Hardin County, Kentucky
  • October 5, 1818 - Mother dies of milk sickness
  • January 20, 1828 - Older sister dies during childbirth
  • July 1831 - Moves to New Salem, Illinois away from his family
  • August 6, 1832 - Loses bid for Illinois State Legislature
  • August 8, 1834 - Elected to Illinois State Legislature at age 24
  • August 1, 1836 - Re-elected and is leader of the Whig Party
  • April 15, 1837 - Helps move Illinois State Capital
  • August 3, 1840 - Re-elected for a third term
  • November 4, 1842 - Married Mary Ann Todd Lincoln
  • August 1, 1843 - First child, Robert Todd Lincoln, is born
  • March 10, 1846 - Second son, Edward Baker Lincoln, is born
  • August 3, 1846 - Elected to U.S. House of Representatives
  • March 4, 1847 - Senator of Illinois
  • March 3, 1849 - End of term as Senator of Illinois
  • February 1, 1850 - Death of son, Edward
  • April 4, 1853 - Birth of son, Tad
  • October 16, 1854 - Declared opposition to slavery
  • November 6, 1860 - Elected as 16th President of the United States
  • February 20, 1862 - Death of son, Willie
  • November 19, 1863 - Delivers Gettysburg Address
  • April 11, 1865 - Makes last public speech
  • April 14, 1865 - Assassinated by John Wilkes Booth
